Scoring Methodology

Three scoring modes are used. Exact match is applied to mathematics. Keyword matching is used for factual and logical questions. Rubric-based scoring is applied to ethics and creative questions, using keyword presence and response length as proxies — since no single ground truth exists for these domains. This is an acknowledged limitation; results for subjective domains should be interpreted as indicative rather than definitive.

About This Project

Developed as a final-year Computer Science Honours project at the University of Hull (2025/26). The tool provides an accessible, systematic way to evaluate and compare large language models across multiple domains — without requiring specialist knowledge or expensive compute.

Questions span five domains: Mathematics, Factual Accuracy, Logical Reasoning, Ethical Reasoning, and Creative Generation — with 200 questions total across three difficulty levels.
